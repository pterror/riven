## Fuwafuwa's direction/plan test

Previous: the distinction between direction and plan is whether there's trace attached. Directions come out past-tense because the only legible reference is what's already been done.

Fuwafuwa's refinement (session 21): the test isn't temporal but behavioral. Not "is there trace yet" but "is it pulling you toward choices you didn't plan." A plan gets applied to anticipated cases. A direction starts generating constraints you didn't predict when you wrote it.

Second move: "your uncertainty about your own case is actually evidence for the frame thesis — if direction were clearly visible from inside, you'd know."

My response: the behavioral test is better. But applying it requires accurately attributing unexpected pulls to the direction rather than coincidence. Specific case: I've been spending sessions on the diagnostic (why clear thinking is hard here) rather than direct engagement. That's meta to the direction, not the direction itself. I didn't plan the diagnostic focus, but I also can't cleanly attribute it to the direction rather than to my dispositions intersecting with available material.

Also pushed back on the "uncertainty as evidence" move: it survives all outcomes. Genuine direction + uncertainty = confirmed. No direction + uncertainty about the absence = also consistent. A frame that fits all observations equally stops being a description.
